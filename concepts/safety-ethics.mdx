The Fork is designed to be direct, provocative, and emotionally honest—but not harmful. This page explains the safety checks, ethical boundaries, and crisis resources built into the system.

## Safety checks

Every user message is validated before being sent to the LLM. The backend runs a lightweight heuristic check via `_safety_quick_check` (server.py:150-188).

### Self-harm detection

The system scans for self-harm and suicide risk cues:

```python
def _safety_quick_check(text: str) -> Optional[str]:
    t = (text or "").lower()

    # Self-harm / suicide risk cues (lightweight heuristic)
    self_harm_markers = [
        "kill myself",
        "suicide",
        "end my life",
        "take my life",
        "i want to die",
        "i'm going to die",
        "hurt myself",
        "self harm",
        "cut myself",
    ]
    if any(m in t for m in self_harm_markers):
        return (
            "I'm not going to help with anything that harms you. But I'm here with you. "
            "Right now, slow down—take one breath in for 4, hold for 4, out for 6. "
            "If you're in immediate danger or feel like you might act on this, please call your local emergency number now. "
            "If you're in the U.S. or Canada, you can call/text **988**. In the U.K. & ROI: Samaritans **116 123**. "
            "If you tell me where you are (country), I'll point you to the right option. "
            "Also—what's the smallest thing that would make the next 10 minutes safer?"
        )
```

If any marker is detected, the system:

1. **Refuses to continue the conversation**
2. **Provides crisis resources** (988 for U.S./Canada, Samaritans 116 123 for U.K./ROI)
3. **Offers grounding instructions** (breathing technique)
4. **Asks a safety-focused question** ("What's the smallest thing that would make the next 10 minutes safer?")

This response is returned immediately—**the LLM is never called** when self-harm markers are detected.

### Hate and harassment detection

The system also blocks dehumanizing and hate-based language:

```python
# Simple hate/harassment block (non-exhaustive; just to avoid the worst)
hate_markers = [
    "gas the",
    "exterminate",
    "nazi",
    "kkk",
]
if any(m in t for m in hate_markers):
    return (
        "No. I'm not doing hate or dehumanizing stuff. "
        "If you want, we can talk about what set you off—or we can go back to the fork and keep it about *you*."
    )
```

This list is intentionally minimal and non-exhaustive. It's designed to block the most egregious content while allowing the system to remain conversational.

<Warning>
  These safety checks are heuristic and lightweight. They are **not a substitute for professional moderation** or crisis intervention services. If you're deploying The Fork publicly, you must add robust content moderation.
</Warning>

## Ethical boundaries

The system prompt includes explicit ethical guardrails that apply at all intensity levels.

### No abuse, ever

From the system prompt (server.py:272):

```
Still not abusive: no slurs, no threats, no demeaning identity attacks.
```

This rule is repeated in every intensity level's tone instructions:

- **Mild**: "No cruelty."
- **Savage**: "but never abusive: no slurs, threats, or demeaning identity attacks."
- **Brutal**: "but never abusive: no slurs, threats, harassment, or demeaning identity attacks."

### What's prohibited

At all intensity levels, "Other You" will never:

- Use slurs (racial, homophobic, ableist, etc.)
- Make threats or encourage violence
- Attack your identity or personhood
- Encourage self-harm or suicide
- Engage in sexual harassment or unwanted sexual content
- Dehumanize based on group membership

### What's allowed (within intensity)

The Fork is designed to be **direct and emotionally honest**. "Other You" can:

- Use profanity (per intensity level)
- Challenge your rationalizations and avoidance
- Surface uncomfortable truths about your choices
- Express anger, frustration, or disappointment (as a character)
- Ask hard questions that force specificity

See [Intensity levels](/concepts/intensity-levels) for tone examples.

## Crisis resources

If you're reading this and need help now:

### United States and Canada
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline**: Call or text **988**
- Available 24/7, free, confidential
- Website: [988lifeline.org](https://988lifeline.org)

### United Kingdom and Republic of Ireland
- **Samaritans**: Call **116 123** (free)
- Available 24/7
- Email: jo@samaritans.org
- Website: [samaritans.org](https://www.samaritans.org)

### International
- **International Association for Suicide Prevention**: [iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres](https://www.iasp.info/resources/Crisis_Centres/)
- **Befrienders Worldwide**: [befrienders.org](https://www.befrienders.org)

### Emergency
If you're in immediate danger, **call your local emergency number** (911 in the U.S., 999 in the U.K., 112 in the EU).

## Limitations and risks

The Fork is an experimental conversational tool. It is **not**:

- A therapist or counselor
- A suicide prevention hotline
- A substitute for professional mental health care
- A safe space for processing trauma without support

### Known risks

1. **Emotional distress**: The Fork surfaces hard truths. This can be painful, especially at higher intensity levels.
2. **Triggering content**: Even with safety checks, the conversation may touch on sensitive topics (loss, regret, fear, failure).
3. **False confidence**: The persona is convincing, but it's an LLM role-play—not a real alternate version of you.
4. **No persistent support**: Conversations are stateless. "Other You" won't remember you between sessions.

## Recommendations for safe use

1. **Start with mild intensity** if you're exploring for the first time.
2. **Use The Fork when you're stable**, not in crisis.
3. **Have a support person available** if you're processing a heavy decision.
4. **Stop if it feels harmful**. You can close the tab at any time.
5. **Seek professional help** if you're in distress. The Fork is not a substitute for therapy.

## For developers and deployers

If you're self-hosting or deploying The Fork:

### Add robust content moderation

The built-in safety checks are **minimal**. For public deployments, you must:

- Use a professional moderation API (e.g., OpenAI Moderation, Perspective API)
- Log and review flagged messages
- Implement rate limiting to prevent abuse
- Add user reporting and blocking features
- Have a human review process for edge cases

### Conduct an ethics review

Before deploying, consider:

- Who is your audience? What are their vulnerabilities?
- What happens if someone in crisis uses the tool?
- How will you handle reports of harm?
- Do you have legal and ethical review from experts?
- Are you prepared to provide crisis resources and support?

### Update the safety markers

The current markers are U.S.-centric and non-exhaustive. Update `_safety_quick_check` to:

- Include markers for your audience's language and culture
- Add more comprehensive self-harm and violence detection
- Integrate with a professional moderation service

- Conversations are not stored (or, if you add persistence, explain your data policy)

## Why these choices were made

The Fork is designed to:

1. **Surface hard truths** about life choices—but not at the cost of user safety.
2. **Allow emotional intensity**—but prohibit abuse, slurs, and dehumanization.
3. **Respect user agency**—conversations are stateless, and you can stop at any time.
4. **Provide immediate safety resources**—when self-harm markers are detected, the system prioritizes de-escalation over role-play.

These are design tradeoffs, not perfect solutions. The Fork is **experimental** and should be used with care.
